Course Description

This HCI course is designed for graduate students to learn how to conduct HCI research. You will learn how to formulate problems into research questions, how to do a literature review, and design and conduct user studies. You will read, present, and discuss state-of-art and classic papers. And ultimately, you will conduct an HCI research project to solve a usability problem as a team.

In this course, you will do the followings:

  • Reading responses: All students will be expected to read the research papers that will be presented in the class and write a three-paragraph report a day before the class. It should include 1) summary of the paper and 2) your reaction to the paper.
    Example from last year
  • Topic presentation: Topic presentation: Once a semester, a team of students will lead a class by summarizing papers and leading discussions on the topic.
  • Assignments: There will be two assignments
  • Course project: There is no better way of learning to conduct HCI research than actually doing one. Each team will 1) find a research topic on building an interactive system that solves a usability problem, 2) do a literature review on the topic, 3) design and implement the system, and 4) evaluate the system. It is encouraged to connect your research to the course project.

While this course welcomes students with a diverse background, you should have good programming skills to develop the software for your project. You can choose any language or computing environment for your project. But I assume that the web is likely the most popular platform for your project, considering the current situation. There will be tutorials on basic web programming for those who are not familiar.
