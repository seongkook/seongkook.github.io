CS 6501: Topics in Human-Computer Interaction

Computers are now taking an essential part of our lives. We use computers in various forms for many tasks such as getting information, communicating with other people, ordering food, or you name it. Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) research is about making the use of computers to be easier, more efficient, more effective, or more accessible. This graduate-level course will help you learn the basic concepts of HCI and how to conduct HCI research, with focuses on novel interaction methods and user interfaces.

In this course, you are expected to do the followings:

  • Reading responses: All students will be expected to read the research papers that will be presented in the class and write a one-page response report before the class. Should include 1) summary of the paper and 2) your reaction to the paper.
  • Topic presentation: Once a semester, each student will lead a class by summarizing papers and leading discussions on the topic.
  • Assignments: There will be two individual assignments.
  • Design Project: There is no better way of learning to conduct HCI research than actually doing one. Each team will 1) find a research topic on building an interactive system that solves a usability problem, 2) design and implement the system, and 3) evaluate the system. It is encouraged to connect your research to the course project.

Topic Presentation Guide

    A student will lead a class by summarizing two papers and leading discussions on the topic.

    If you’re leading the class,

  • You’ll summarize two papers for 30 minutes (max 40 min).
    Summarize the goal of the papers and methods they used to achieve the goals. You don’t need to blend two papers when presenting. But have a moment that summarizes and compares two papers before or after explaining the two papers.
  • You’ll lead discussions for 10 - 20 minutes. Not just answering questions, you may lead discussions about its uses, limitations, or future directions.
  • You don’t need to write a reading response for the week you’re presenting.

    • If you're participating in the class,

  • Read the reading materials. While the leading student will present the paper, it’ll be hard for you to participate in a deep discussion if you haven’t read the papers.
  • Submit a reading response for the week. You will pick one of the four papers that will be presented in the next week and submit a reading response by Monday of the week the paper will be presented.
  • Actively participate in the discussion. Discussion is a very important part of learning about a topic. Ask good questions and speak out your suggestions.
