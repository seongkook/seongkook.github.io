CS 6205: Research Methods in Human-Computer Interaction

Fall 2025


This course is designed for graduate students to learn how to conduct HCI research. You will learn to formulate problems into research questions, to do a literature review, to design and conduct experiments. You will read, present, and discuss state-of-art and classic papers. And ultimately, you will conduct an HCI research project to solve a usability problem as a team and write an HCI paper.

This was previously CS6501: Special Topics in Human-Computer Interaction and just given a permanent course number assigned. If you have taken that course, you do not need to take this course.

Course Description

The best way to learn to conduct research is to do it.Therefore, in this course, you will do the following activities that are essential in HCI research:

  • Reading responses: Knowing literature is the key in research! You will read a research paper and write a two-paragraph reading response each week. The reading response will include 1) summary of the paper and 2) your reaction to the paper.
    I understand there are rainy days, so two lowest-grade reading responses will not count towards your grade.
  • Assignment: You will learn to design and conduct experiments, and you'll try yourself one as an assignment.
  • Quizzes: There will be three simple quizzes so that you can remind yourself about what you've learned in the class.
  • Summary presentation: Once a semester, you will give a 5-minute presentation about a paper before in-class paper discussion.
  • Course project: This is the essense of this course. You will conduct an HCI project as a team. Each team will 1) find a research topic on building an interactive system that solves a usability problem, 2) do a literature review on the topic, 3) design and implement the system, and 4) evaluate the system. It is encouraged to connect your research to the course project.

While this course welcomes students with a diverse background, you should be able to write code for successful completion of the project.

Use of GenAI in This Course

You may use AI for getting help with reports and assignments. There are two requirements:

  • You take the full responsibility on whatever is on your submission. If there are false information caused by AI hallucination, it will be on you.
  • Write how you used AI in your report, along with prompts you used. For example, "I used ChatGPT to turn my long writing into a single-paragraph summary." or, "I used Google Gemini to check for grammar", "We used Google Stitch to create the user interface of our project system."
If you fail to meet any of the two requirements above, you will get 0 for that assignment.
